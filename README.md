### 👥 Technical Leadership
Technical leadership isn't about being the smartest person in the room, but rather serving as an **effective translator** between different teams and perspectives. A lead developer's primary role involves:

- 🌉 **Bridging communication gaps** between backend developers, frontend teams, and product managers
- 🎯 **Keeping everyone focused** on solving the actual problem rather than getting lost in technical debates
- ❓ **Asking the right questions** and admitting when you don't know something
- 🤝 **Creating space for experts** to contribute their best work rather than trying to out-expert them

## 🤖 Perspective on AI in Development

> **"AI as a Force Multiplier, Not a Replacement"**

AI tools are creating a **productivity gap** between senior and junior developers rather than democratizing coding. While AI amplifies senior engineers' expertise by automating boilerplate and accelerating prototyping, it often becomes a liability for juniors who lack the experience to evaluate AI-generated code quality.

**Key Observations:**
- ⚠️ Companies replacing seniors with junior+AI teams are experiencing increased technical debt, security vulnerabilities, and production failures
- ✅ The key is using AI as a **force multiplier for existing expertise** rather than a replacement for fundamental knowledge
- 🎓 Experience and deep understanding remain irreplaceable in software development
